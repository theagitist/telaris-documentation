# Glossary

**Page.** A single Hotglue canvas: the surface you arrange objects on. A page can be attached to a wormhole so the wormhole shows it.

**Object.** Anything you place on a page: an image, a text box, a video, a drawing. Each object can be moved, resized, layered, locked, linked, and deleted.

**NEW menu.** The menu for adding objects to a page. Open it by single-clicking the empty background, or with <kbd>alt</kbd> + <kbd>o</kbd>.

**PAGE menu.** The menu for whole-page settings (background, grid). Open it by double-clicking the empty background, or with <kbd>alt</kbd> + <kbd>p</kbd>.

**Context menu.** The fan of small tool icons that appears around an object when you select it. Its tools depend on the object's type.

**Grid.** The invisible spacing objects snap to as you move and resize them. Adjustable and hideable from the PAGE menu; hold <kbd>ctrl</kbd> to ignore it for one move.

**Revisions.** The history of a page. Open it with the **Revisions** button in the editor. Hotglue snapshots a page about once an hour while it is edited and keeps the snapshots for seven days. Revisions stand in for an undo feature, since there is none.

**Soundboard.** A per-page mode (toggled from the PAGE menu) that lets a visitor tap video tiles to play clips, with self-hosted clips mixing through a shared audio engine so several can sound at once.

**Wormhole.** In Telaris, the smallest unit of content. A Hotglue page can be attached to a wormhole so the wormhole shows your freeform layout instead of its classic media.

**Galaxy.** In Telaris, a cluster of wormholes. Your edit access to a galaxy (read-write, read-only, or none) determines which of its wormholes' Hotglue pages you can change.

**Classic media.** A wormhole's plain, stacked media layout, the alternative to a Hotglue page. A wormhole shows whichever of the two (Classic or Hotglue) is selected when it is saved.

## Not available in this build

The Telaris build of Hotglue intentionally leaves out the features that would let you paste in arbitrary HTML or scripts (the "code" and "user code" modules). If you have used another copy of Hotglue that had them, that is the difference. Everything you add here is one of the content types in *Adding content*.
