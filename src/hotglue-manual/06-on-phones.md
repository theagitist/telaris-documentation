# How it looks on phones

A Hotglue page is laid out at whatever size you build it. Visitors, though, arrive on screens of every width, and many of them on phones. This chapter explains what happens to your layout on a small screen, and how to author with that in mind.

> [!note] Viewing versus editing
> This chapter is about **viewing** a finished page on a small screen, which works well. **Editing** a page is different: do that on a computer or tablet, as noted in *Getting in*. The two are separate experiences.

## What a visitor sees on a small screen

When a published page is wider than the screen showing it, Hotglue shrinks the whole page to fit the screen's width, keeping every object in the same relative position. Your careful arrangement is preserved; it just appears smaller. This shrinking only goes so far: below a minimum scale, Hotglue stops shrinking and lets the page scroll sideways instead, so the page never becomes too tiny to read.

This happens automatically and only on the published view. It never changes anything in the editor, and it never alters your saved layout. You always edit at full size.

![A published Hotglue page on a phone-width screen: the whole layout scaled down to fit, with every object in the same arrangement](assets/images/hotglue-manual/06-on-phone.png)

## Authoring tips for phones

Because the page is scaled as one piece rather than reflowed, a few habits make a layout that survives the shrink:

- **Keep text large enough to read after shrinking.** If a block of text is small on a wide page, it will be very small on a phone. When in doubt, make it bigger.
- **Avoid an unnecessarily wide page** if a phone audience matters. The wider the page, the more it has to shrink to fit, and the smaller everything becomes. A narrower page shrinks less.
- **Group related objects** so they stay legible together when scaled, rather than spreading a single idea across the full width of a very wide canvas.
- **Preview on a phone.** Open the published page (not the editor) on an actual phone, or narrow your browser window, to see the scaled result. Use **View in browser** from the Hotglue content view.

> [!tip] Wide and tall is fine, just deliberate
> Hotglue is happy with very large pages, and a sprawling canvas can be exactly the point. Just remember that the bigger the page, the more a phone has to shrink it. Decide the trade-off on purpose rather than by accident.
