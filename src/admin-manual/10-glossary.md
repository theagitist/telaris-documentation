# Glossary

Terms an administrator meets in the console and in this manual. Where a term also appears in the Editor Manual, the meaning is the same; this glossary leans toward the administrator's side of it.

**Administrator**. An account that can reach the Admin Console and manage the instance. Distinct from an editor, who authors content but cannot reach the console. Chapter 1.

**Admission**. The Pluriverse operator's decision to admit an applying instance into the federation directory. Chapter 7.

**Allow editors**. The instance-wide switch, in Global Settings, that enables or freezes all editing at once. The top of the editing cascade. Chapters 4 and 6.

**API key**. The key protecting the instance's internal content API. A default one exists on a normally set-up instance; managed on the API Keys tab. Chapter 9.

**Backup**. A downloadable file capturing the instance's content, kept off the server for recovery or migration. Distinct from a snapshot. Chapter 8.

**Blocking**. Severing a peer relationship: mirrors dropped, publications withdrawn, sharing stopped in both directions. Uniform and deliberate. Chapter 7.

**Cluster**. A named group of galaxies. A level in the editing cascade and a scope for keyword matching. Chapter 5.

**Editing cascade**. The instance / cluster / galaxy / user levels at which editing can be switched off, widest turning off everything inside it unless something narrower turns it back on. Administrators are exempt. Chapter 4.

**Editor**. An account that authors content within the access it has been granted, and cannot reach the console. Chapter 1.

**Federation**. A family of independent instances that can trust one another and share galaxies, coordinated by a Pluriverse, with no instance in charge. Chapter 7.

**Fuzzy matching**. An optional, per-instance or per-cluster loosening of keyword matching so near-spellings connect, not only identical words. Off by default. Chapter 5.

**Imported galaxy**. A read-only mirror of a galaxy published by a peer, present on your instance while you subscribe to it. Refreshable; never editable here. Chapters 5 and 7.

**Mail settings**. The SMTP configuration, in Global Settings, that lets the instance send email. The password is the one secret never included in a backup. Chapter 6.

**Personal galaxy**. A galaxy created for an editor, named from their name by the self-enrolment naming choice. Prompted about when the account is deleted. Chapters 2 and 3.

**Pluriverse**. The central site that coordinates a federation: it keeps the directory of member instances and helps them find and trust one another. A coordinator, not an owner. Chapter 7.

**Publish**. Offering one of your galaxies to a trusted peer so they may mirror it. Per galaxy and per peer; retractable. Chapter 7.

**Read-only / read-write seat**. An editor's access level on a galaxy: read-write can change it, read-only can only view it in the editor. Imported galaxies are read-only to everyone. Chapter 4.

**Schema-mismatch banner**. The console warning that the code and the database structure disagree, a signal a deploy did not finish, not a routine state. Chapters 8 and 9.

**Self-service enrolment**. The optional feature letting people request an editor account themselves, gated by a cap, an email-domain allowlist, and email confirmation. Off by default. Chapter 3.

**Snapshot**. A copy of the instance's content the instance keeps for you, on demand or on a schedule, restorable in place. The everyday safety net. Chapter 8.

**Subscribe**. Pulling a read-only mirror of a galaxy a peer has published to you. Chapter 7.

**Trust**. A mutual, symmetric relationship between two instances, established by a handshake, that makes sharing possible but shares nothing on its own. Chapter 7.

**Vetting**. Marking a self-enrolled editor as recognised: it lets them set a password and tells them they are recognised, but does not gate editing. Chapter 3.
